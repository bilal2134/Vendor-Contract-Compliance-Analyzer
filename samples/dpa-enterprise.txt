This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") supplements and forms part of
the Master Service Agreement dated February 1, 2026 ("MSA") between
the parties. This DPA establishes the terms and conditions under which
NovaTech Solutions will process Personal Data on behalf of Greybeard
Corporate Solutions in performance of the contracted services.

The parties acknowledge that: (a) the Company acts as the Controller
(Data Controller under GDPR; Business under CCPA) and determines the
purposes and means of processing; (b) Vendor acts as the Processor
(Data Processor under GDPR; Service Provider under CCPA) and processes
Personal Data only on the Company's instructions.

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
ARTICLE 1 – DEFINITIONS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

For purposes of this DPA:

"Controller" means Greybeard Corporate Solutions acting in its capacity
as the entity determining the purposes and means of processing.

"Data Subject" means an identified or identifiable natural person to
whom Personal Data relates.

"EEA" means the European Economic Area.

"GDPR" means EU General Data Protection Regulation 2016/679, as
directly applicable or as implemented into applicable national law.

"Personal Data" means any information relating to an identified or
identifiable natural person, as defined under applicable privacy law,
including GDPR, CCPA, PIPEDA, and the UK Data Protection Act 2018.

"Processing" means any operation performed on Personal Data including
collection, storage, retrieval, use, disclosure, or erasure.

"Processor" means NovaTech Solutions acting in its capacity as the
entity processing Personal Data on behalf of the Controller.

"SCCs" means the Standard Contractual Clauses for the transfer of
personal data to third countries, as adopted by the European Commission
on June 4, 2021 (Modules 2 and 3 as applicable).

"Sensitive Personal Data" means Personal Data revealing racial or
ethnic origin, political opinions, religious or philosophical beliefs,
trade union membership, genetic data, biometric data, health data, and
data concerning sex life or sexual orientation.

ARTICLE 2 – DETAILS OF PROCESSING
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

2.1  Subject Matter.  Processor will process Personal Data to provide
the technology and professional services described in the MSA and
associated Statements of Work.

2.2  Duration.  Processing will occur during the term of the MSA and
for such additional period as is strictly necessary to fulfil
post-termination obligations (including data retention, deletion, and
audit), not to exceed 30 days post-termination.

2.3  Nature and Purpose.  Processor will process Personal Data to:
  (a)  Provide software-as-a-service analytics and workflow automation;
  (b)  Provide technical support, maintenance, and professional services;
  (c)  Generate usage analytics and system performance reports for
       the Company's internal business operations;
  (d)  Perform data integration, transformation, and reporting services
       as specified in each SOW.

2.4  Categories of Data Subjects.
  - Company employees, contractors, and former employees
  - Company customers and end-users
  - Company suppliers and business partners (where applicable)
  - Company prospects and leads (where applicable)

2.5  Categories of Personal Data Processed.
  - Contact information (name, email, phone, address)
  - Employment data (role, department, employee ID, compensation bands)
  - Authentication data (usernames, hashed passwords, MFA tokens)
  - Transaction and usage data (system logs, access records)
  - Financial data (invoice records, cost center allocations)
  - Communication data (support tickets, correspondence)

2.6  Sensitive Personal Data.  The services as designed do not
contemplate routine processing of Sensitive Personal Data. If any
Sensitive Personal Data is inadvertently included in a dataset, Vendor
must notify the Company immediately and await further instructions before
processing.

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
ARTICLE 3 – PROCESSOR OBLIGATIONS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

3.1  General Processing Obligations.  Processor shall:
  (a)  Process Personal Data only on documented instructions from the
       Controller, unless required to do otherwise by applicable law
       (in which case Processor shall inform Controller before processing
       unless prohibited by law);
  (b)  Ensure that personnel authorized to process Personal Data are
       bound by appropriate confidentiality obligations;
  (c)  Implement the technical and organizational security measures
       described in Annex II;
  (d)  Respect the conditions in Article 4 for engaging sub-processors;
  (e)  Assist the Controller in ensuring compliance with Data Subject
       rights requests as described in Article 6;
  (f)  Make available all information necessary to demonstrate compliance
       with GDPR Article 28 and cooperate with audits as specified in
       Article 10;
  (g)  Immediately inform the Controller if any instruction infringes
       GDPR or applicable privacy laws.

3.2  Purpose Limitation.  Processor may not process Company Personal
Data for any purpose other than performing services under the MSA. In
particular, Processor shall not: (a) sell or rent Personal Data;
(b) use Personal Data for targeted advertising; (c) build user profiles
beyond what is necessary for service delivery; (d) disclose Personal
Data to third parties for their independent business purposes.

3.3  Data Minimization.  Processor shall collect and process only the
minimum Personal Data necessary to perform the contracted services.
Any excessive data collected must be deleted promptly upon discovery.

3.4  Data Quality.  Processor shall implement appropriate measures to
maintain the accuracy, completeness, and timeliness of Personal Data
processed on behalf of the Controller.

ARTICLE 4 – SUB-PROCESSORS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

4.1  Authorization.  Controller provides general authorization for
Processor to engage sub-processors subject to the conditions in this
Article 4.

4.2  Current Sub-Processors.  As of the effective date of this DPA,
Processor's approved sub-processors for processing Controller's Personal
Data are listed in Annex III. Processor will maintain this list and
make it available on request.

4.3  New Sub-Processors.  Processor shall provide Controller with at
least 30 days written notice (email to privacy@greybeard.com) before
engaging any new sub-processor. Controller may object to the new
sub-processor within 14 days of notice; if Controller objects and
Processor cannot provide the services without the new sub-processor,
Controller may terminate the affected SOW without penalty.

4.4  Flow-Down Obligations.  Processor shall impose on each sub-processor
contractual data protection obligations that are at least as stringent
as those imposed on Processor under this DPA. Processor remains fully
liable to Controller for any sub-processor's failure to comply.

4.5  Sub-Processor Audit.  Processor shall conduct or obtain annual
assessments of each sub-processor's security and compliance posture and
make assessment results available to Controller on request.

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
ARTICLE 5 – INTERNATIONAL TRANSFERS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

5.1  Restriction on International Transfers.  Processor shall not
transfer any EEA-origin Personal Data outside the EEA without a lawful
transfer mechanism as specified in Section 5.2.

5.2  Transfer Mechanisms.  Where Processor transfers EEA Personal Data
to countries not recognized as providing adequate protection, transfers
shall rely on:
  (a)  EU Standard Contractual Clauses (Module 2: Controller-to-Processor)
       as incorporated in Annex IV to this DPA; or
  (b)  An adequacy decision by the European Commission; or
  (c)  Binding Corporate Rules approved by a competent supervisory authority.

5.3  Transfer Impact Assessment.  Processor shall maintain and provide
to Controller, upon request, a Transfer Impact Assessment (TIA) covering
each country where it or its sub-processors process EEA Personal Data.
The TIA must be reviewed and updated following changes in applicable law
or sub-processor infrastructure.

5.4  UK GDPR.  For UK-origin Personal Data, transfers outside the UK
shall rely on UK SCCs (IDTA or addendum to EU SCCs) or an adequacy
regulation confirmed by the UK Secretary of State.

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
ARTICLE 6 – DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

6.1  Support for Rights Requests.  Processor shall assist Controller
in fulfilling Data Subject requests under applicable privacy law,
including GDPR Articles 15-22 (access, rectification, erasure,
restriction, portability, and objection) and CCPA Sections 1798.100–
1798.198 (know, delete, correct, and opt-out of sale).

6.2  Response Timeline.  Upon Controller's written notification,
Processor shall provide the information or take the action necessary for
Controller to respond to a Data Subject request within 72 hours of such
notification.

6.3  System Capability.  Processor represents and warrants that its
systems are technically capable of: (a) identifying all Personal Data
associated with a specific Data Subject by email, name, or customer ID;
(b) exporting that data in a machine-readable format (JSON or CSV);
(c) permanently deleting that data and all backups upon instruction.
Processor shall provide evidence of these capabilities in the form of
technical documentation within 30 days of this DPA's effective date.

6.4  Direct Requests.  If a Data Subject contacts Processor directly
(rather than Controller) with a rights request, Processor shall promptly
forward the request to Controller's DPO within 24 hours without acting
on it unilaterally.

ARTICLE 7 – SECURITY MEASURES
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

7.1  Security Obligations.  Processor shall implement and maintain
technical and organizational measures ("TOMs") commensurate with the
risk to Data Subjects' rights and freedoms, having regard to the state
of the art, costs, and the nature of processing. Minimum TOMs are
described in Annex II and include:
  (a)  Encryption of Personal Data in transit (TLS 1.2+) and at rest
       (AES-256);
  (b)  Multi-factor authentication for all systems processing Personal Data;
  (c)  Role-based access control with least privilege enforcement;
  (d)  Regular vulnerability assessments and penetration testing;
  (e)  Security information and event management (SIEM) monitoring;
  (f)  Secure software development lifecycle (SSDLC) practices.

7.2  Security Certifications.  Processor maintains:
  (a)  SOC 2 Type II (Trust Services Criteria: Security, Availability,
       Confidentiality) — current report period October 2024 – September 2025;
  (b)  ISO 27001:2022 — Certificate IS-2023-847291 valid through April 2026.
  Processor must maintain these certifications in good standing throughout
  the term and promptly notify Controller if any certification lapses.

7.3  Security Updates.  Processor shall provide the Company with its
most current security certifications within 5 business days of any
request. Processor shall notify Company of any qualification or exception
in a SOC 2 report, together with a management remediation plan, within
10 business days of the report's issue date.

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
ARTICLE 8 – DATA BREACH NOTIFICATION
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

8.1  Notification Timeline.  Without undue delay and in any event within
4 hours of becoming aware of a Data Breach (or suspected Data Breach)
involving Controller's Personal Data, Processor shall notify Controller's
Data Protection Officer (privacy@greybeard.com) and CISO
(ciso@greybeard.com) by telephone and email.

8.2  Incident Report Contents.  Within 24 hours of initial notification,
Processor shall provide a written incident report containing:
  (a)  Description of the nature of the breach (unauthorized access,
       disclosure, loss, destruction);
  (b)  Categories and approximate number of individuals affected;
  (c)  Categories and approximate number of records concerned;
  (d)  Contact details of Processor's DPO (dpo@novatechsolutions.com);
  (e)  Likely consequences of the breach;
  (f)  Measures taken or proposed to contain, investigate, and remediate;
  (g)  Timeline of discovered events.

8.3  Cooperation.  Processor shall cooperate fully with Controller in
investigating the breach, notifying affected Data Subjects and
supervisory authorities, and remediating root causes.

8.4  Non-Disclosure.  Processor shall not publicly disclose a Data
Breach affecting Controller's Personal Data without Controller's prior
written approval, except as required by applicable law.

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
ARTICLE 9 – DATA RETENTION AND DELETION
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

9.1  Retention During Engagement.  Processor shall retain Personal Data
only for the duration necessary to provide the contracted services and
in accordance with the Company's Data Retention Schedule (DRS) provided
at contract execution, as updated from time to time with notice.

9.2  Retention Schedule Applicability.  The following retention periods
apply to Company Personal Data categories:
  - Employee records:           7 years post-employment or post-termination
                                of this Agreement, whichever is longer
  - Transaction / usage logs:   3 years from date of creation
  - Financial records:          7 years from fiscal year end
  - Security incident records:  5 years from incident closure
  - Backup media:               Overwritten on next scheduled backup cycle,
                                maximum 90 days

9.3  Post-Termination Deletion.  Upon expiration or termination of the
MSA, Processor shall, within 30 calendar days of the termination
effective date:
  (a)  Return all Personal Data to Controller in a structured, commonly
       used, machine-readable format (CSV, JSON, or SQL export as agreed);
  (b)  Certifiably destroy all remaining copies of Personal Data from
       production systems, test environments, and offline storage;
  (c)  Ensure all sub-processors have similarly destroyed or returned
       Personal Data; and
  (d)  Provide the Company with a signed Certificate of Data Destruction
       within 5 business days of completing deletion, signed by
       Processor's Data Protection Officer.

9.4  Destruction Standards.  Data destruction shall comply with
NIST SP 800-88 Rev. 1 (Clear, Purge, or Destroy standards for electronic
and physical media). Certificates of Destruction must identify the
methods used, media types, and the authorizing DPO.

9.5  Legal Hold Exception.  If Processor receives a legal hold notice
from Controller, Processor shall suspend destruction of the specified
data until the hold is formally lifted by Controller's General Counsel
in writing, notwithstanding any retention schedule or post-termination
deletion obligation.

ARTICLE 10 – AUDIT RIGHTS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

10.1  Audit Right.  Controller, or a third-party auditor appointed by
Controller, shall have the right to audit Processor's compliance with
this DPA upon 30 days written notice, no more than once per calendar year.
In the event of a confirmed Data Breach or material compliance concern,
Controller may request an audit on 5 business days notice.

10.2  Audit Scope.  Audits may cover: data processing records, security
controls, sub-processor contracts, retention and deletion records, and
incident response documentation.

10.3  Evidence in Lieu of Audit.  Processor may satisfy audit
obligations by providing: current SOC 2 Type II reports, ISO 27001
surveillance audit certificates, and completed annual risk assessment
questionnaires, provided these are materially complete and current.

10.4  Cooperation.  Processor shall provide reasonable access to
personnel, documentation, and systems necessary to conduct any audit.
Processor may require auditors to execute a non-disclosure agreement
consistent with Article 5 of the MSA before commencing on-site work.

ARTICLE 11 – INSURANCE
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

11.1  Cyber Liability Insurance.  Processor shall maintain Cyber
Liability insurance as required by Section 9.1 of the MSA and the
Company's Procurement Playbook (Section 7.1.2). This DPA confirms
that Processor's cyber liability coverage of USD $5,000,000 per
occurrence and USD $5,000,000 aggregate applies to data breach events
involving Controller's Personal Data. This coverage is documented in
the Insurance Certificate attached to the MSA as Exhibit C.

COMPLIANCE NOTE: The Company's Playbook (§7.1.2) requires USD $10,000,000
aggregate for Tier 1 vendors. Processor is currently classified as Tier 2,
making USD $5,000,000 aggregate the applicable threshold. Should Processor
be reclassified to Tier 1, enhanced cyber coverage must be obtained within
60 days of reclassification notice. This gap is recorded in Exception Log
EX-2024-113.

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
ARTICLE 12 – LIABILITY
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

12.1  Processor Liability.  Processor shall be liable to Controller
for damages caused by Processing in breach of this DPA. Liability is
not subject to the general aggregate cap in MSA Article 8 for breaches
attributable to Processor's deliberate violation of data protection law.

12.2  Controller Liability.  Controller shall be liable to Processor
for damages caused by Controller's processing instructions that violate
applicable privacy law.

12.3  Apportionment.  Where damages are caused jointly by Controller
and Processor, each party's liability shall be apportioned according to
fault.

ARTICLE 13 – GENERAL PROVISIONS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

13.1  Order of Precedence.  This DPA shall prevail over the MSA with
respect to the processing of Personal Data. Where both documents are
silent, applicable privacy law governs.

13.2  Regulatory Changes.  If applicable privacy laws change materially,
the parties shall negotiate in good faith to amend this DPA within 60 days
of the effective date of the legislative change.

13.3  Governing Law.  This DPA shall be governed by the laws of the
State of Delaware, without prejudice to Data Subjects' rights under
applicable privacy law.

13.4  Entire Agreement.  This DPA, together with its Annexes and the
MSA, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties with respect
to the processing of Personal Data.

ANNEX II – TECHNICAL AND ORGANIZATIONAL SECURITY MEASURES (TOMs)
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1.  Access Control
    - Role-based access control (RBAC) aligned to job function
    - Principle of least privilege enforced
    - Privileged access management (PAM) for administrative accounts
    - Quarterly access reviews
    - FIDO2 / TOTP MFA on all production access

2.  Encryption
    - In transit: TLS 1.3 (minimum TLS 1.2); TLS 1.0 / 1.1 disabled
    - At rest: AES-256-GCM with FIPS 140-2 Level 2 HSM key management
    - Key rotation: annually for data encryption keys; immediately upon
      compromise suspicion

3.  Logging and Monitoring
    - SIEM platform (Splunk Enterprise Security) collecting logs from
      all production systems, IAM, and network infrastructure
    - Log retention: 3 years for security logs; searchable online for 1 year
    - 24/7 Security Operations Center (SOC) with 15-minute alert response SLA

4.  Vulnerability Management
    - Weekly authenticated vulnerability scans (Qualys)
    - Annual external penetration test (independent third party)
    - Semi-annual web application penetration test (OWASP Top 10 scope)
    - CVSS 9.0+ patches applied within 14 days; CVSS 7.0–8.9 within 30 days

5.  Incident Response
    - Documented IRP reviewed annually
    - Annual tabletop exercise; records available to Company on request
    - 4-hour breach notification SLA (Article 8 of this DPA)

6.  Physical Security
    - Data centers: SOC 2 Type II certified co-location facilities
    - Badge access with biometric verification
    - CCTV monitoring, visitor logging

7.  Data Disposal
    - Production: cryptographic erasure + logical deletion
    - Physical media: NIST SP 800-88 (Purge or Destroy)
    - Certificates of Destruction provided as required
