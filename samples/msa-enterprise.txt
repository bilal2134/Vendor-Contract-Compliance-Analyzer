WHEREAS, the Company desires to engage Vendor to provide certain
technology and professional services as described in one or more
Statements of Work ("SOWs") to be entered into from time to time;

WHEREAS, Vendor has the expertise, personnel, and infrastructure to
provide such services in accordance with applicable quality, security,
and compliance standards;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants herein and
other good and valuable consideration, the parties agree as follows:

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
ARTICLE 1 – SERVICES AND DELIVERABLES
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1.1  Services.  Vendor shall perform the services described in each
SOW executed by authorized representatives of both parties. Each SOW
shall be incorporated by reference and form part of this Agreement.

1.2  Change Orders.  Any modification to agreed scope, timeline, or
fees requires a written Change Order signed by both parties. Vendor
shall not commence out-of-scope work without an executed Change Order.

1.3  Personnel.  Vendor shall assign qualified personnel to perform
the services. Key personnel identified in an SOW may not be replaced
or reassigned without 15 days advance written notice to the Company and
Company's written approval, which shall not be unreasonably withheld.

1.4  Subcontracting.  Vendor shall not subcontract any portion of the
services without prior written consent of the Company. Currently
approved subcontractors are listed in Exhibit D. All subcontractors
must be bound by obligations no less restrictive than those applicable
to Vendor under this Agreement.

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
ARTICLE 2 – TERM AND TERMINATION
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

2.1  Initial Term.  This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and
continues for an initial term of 36 months ("Initial Term") unless
earlier terminated in accordance with this Article 2.

2.2  Renewal.  This Agreement shall automatically renew for successive
12-month periods ("Renewal Terms") unless either party provides written
notice of its intent not to renew at least 90 days before the expiration
of the then-current term.

2.3  Termination for Convenience.  Either party may terminate this
Agreement or any SOW without cause by providing 60 days prior written
notice to the other party. Upon receipt of such notice, Vendor shall
continue providing services during the notice period unless otherwise
directed by the Company. Fees for services properly rendered during
the notice period shall be payable.

2.4  Termination for Cause.  Either party may terminate this Agreement
if the other party commits a material breach and fails to cure such
breach within 30 days of receiving written notice specifying the breach
in reasonable detail.

2.5  Termination for Insolvency.  Either party may terminate this
Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other party: (a)
becomes insolvent; (b) makes a general assignment for the benefit of
creditors; (c) has a receiver or trustee appointed; or (d) files or
has filed against it a petition under applicable bankruptcy or
insolvency law that is not dismissed within 60 days.

2.6  Change of Control.  Vendor shall provide 90 days advance written
notice of any anticipated change of control event. If the acquirer is a
direct competitor of the Company as identified in Exhibit E (Competitor
List), the Company may terminate this Agreement without penalty within
60 days of change of control notice.

2.7  Effect of Termination; Exit Assistance.  Upon termination or
expiration of this Agreement: (a) Vendor shall provide up to 90 days
of transition assistance at no additional charge upon request; (b) all
licenses granted by the Company to Vendor terminate immediately; (c)
each party shall return or destroy the other party's Confidential
Information within 30 days of the termination effective date; (d) Vendor
shall provide a Certificate of Data Destruction within 5 business days
of completing data disposal.

NOTE: The Company's standard termination-for-convenience notice period
is 90 days as specified in the Procurement Policy Playbook Section 14.1.
This Agreement reflects a negotiated 60-day period for operational
flexibility. This deviation is recorded in Exception Log EX-2024-113
and approved by CPO and Legal. Inconsistency with the 90-day Playbook
standard is acknowledged and approved.

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
ARTICLE 3 – FEES AND PAYMENT
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

3.1  Fees.  The Company shall pay Vendor the fees specified in each SOW.
Unless otherwise stated in an SOW, fees quoted are in US Dollars and
exclusive of applicable taxes.

3.2  Invoicing and Payment.  Vendor shall issue invoices monthly in
arrears. Invoices must reference the Company purchase order number and
applicable SOW number. The Company shall pay undisputed invoices within
Net-45 days of receipt.

3.3  Late Payment.  Amounts undisputed and unpaid after Net-45 days
shall accrue interest at 1.0% per month or the maximum rate permitted
by applicable law, whichever is lower.

3.4  Taxes.  Each party is responsible for its own income taxes.
Vendor shall be responsible for collecting and remitting applicable
sales or use taxes unless the Company provides a valid exemption
certificate.

3.5  Price Adjustment.  Fixed fees are not subject to unilateral
adjustment during any SOW term. Time-and-materials rates may be
adjusted annually on 90 days written notice, subject to a cap of
CPI (All Urban Consumers) + 3 percentage points per annum.

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
ARTICLE 4 – INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

4.1  Work Product.  All custom deliverables created by Vendor
specifically for the Company under a SOW shall be works-made-for-hire
owned by the Company. Where any deliverable does not qualify as a
work-made-for-hire under applicable law, Vendor hereby irrevocably
assigns all right, title, and interest to the Company.

4.2  Background IP.  Vendor retains ownership of its pre-existing
intellectual property ("Background IP"). Vendor grants the Company a
non-exclusive, perpetual, royalty-free, irrevocable license to use any
Background IP embedded in deliverables to the extent necessary to use
the deliverables as intended.

4.3  Open Source.  Vendor shall provide a complete bill of materials
listing all open source software components in any deliverable. Vendor
warrants that no open source component imposes obligations inconsistent
with the Company's commercial use, including no requirement to disclose
proprietary source code (copyleft contamination).

4.4  IP Indemnification.  Vendor shall indemnify and hold harmless the
Company against third-party claims alleging that Vendor deliverables
infringe any third party's intellectual property rights (patent,
copyright, trademark, or trade secret).

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
ARTICLE 5 – CONFIDENTIALITY
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

5.1  Obligations.  Each party ("Recipient") shall: (a) hold the other
party's Confidential Information in strict confidence using at least the
same degree of care it uses for its own confidential information of
similar sensitivity but no less than reasonable care; (b) use
Confidential Information solely to perform obligations or exercise
rights under this Agreement; (c) disclose Confidential Information only
to employees, agents, and subcontractors who have a need to know and
are bound by written confidentiality obligations at least as stringent
as those in this Article 5.

5.2  Exceptions.  Confidentiality obligations do not apply to
information that: (a) is or becomes publicly known through no fault of
the Recipient; (b) was known to Recipient before disclosure without
restriction; (c) is independently developed by Recipient without use of
Confidential Information; (d) is required to be disclosed by applicable
law or court order (with prior notice to Disclosing Party where lawfully
permitted).

5.3  Survival.  Confidentiality obligations survive termination or
expiration of this Agreement for five (5) years.

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
ARTICLE 6 – DATA PROTECTION
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

6.1  DPA Incorporation.  The parties have executed a separate Data
Processing Agreement ("DPA") dated February 1, 2026, which is
incorporated by reference and forms part of this Agreement. In the
event of conflict between this Agreement and the DPA regarding
the processing of Personal Data, the DPA shall govern.

6.2  Data Security.  Vendor shall implement and maintain technical and
organizational security measures sufficient to protect Company Data
against unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction,
consistent with the requirements of the DPA and Exhibit A (Security
Standards).

6.3  Security Certifications.  Vendor represents and warrants that it
maintains a current SOC 2 Type II report issued by an accredited
independent auditor. Vendor's current SOC 2 report (Period: October 1,
2024 – September 30, 2025) is attached as Exhibit B. Vendor also holds
ISO 27001:2022 certification, certificate number IS-2023-847291, valid
through April 30, 2026. Vendor shall renew its ISO 27001 certification
and provide updated certification evidence to the Company within
15 business days of renewal.

6.4  Breach Notification.  Vendor shall notify the Company within
4 hours of discovering a Data Breach or suspected Data Breach involving
Company Data in accordance with DPA Section 6.

ARTICLE 7 – REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

7.1  Vendor Warranties.
  (a)  Professional Standards: Services will be performed in a
       professionally skillful manner consistent with industry standards.
  (b)  Compliance: Vendor and its personnel will comply with all
       applicable laws and regulations governing performance of the services.
  (c)  No Conflicts: Execution and performance of this Agreement does not
       conflict with any other contract or obligation of Vendor.
  (d)  Financial Stability: Vendor is a validly existing entity in good
       standing and has no pending insolvency proceedings.
  (e)  No Malware: Deliverables will not contain any malware, virus,
       backdoor, or unauthorized data collection mechanism.
  (f)  Security Certifications: Vendor will maintain required security
       certifications throughout the term and promptly notify the Company
       if any certification lapses.

7.2  Mutual Warranties.  Each party warrants that: (a) it has full legal
authority to execute this Agreement; (b) the person executing this
Agreement is duly authorized; (c) it will comply with applicable laws.

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
ARTICLE 8 – LIMITATION OF LIABILITY
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

8.1  Consequential Damages Waiver.  EXCEPT FOR BREACHES OF ARTICLE 5
(CONFIDENTIALITY), INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY INFRINGEMENT, GROSS
NEGLIGENCE, OR WILLFUL MISCONDUCT, NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE FOR
INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, PUNITIVE, OR EXEMPLARY
DAMAGES, INCLUDING LOSS OF PROFITS OR BUSINESS OPPORTUNITIES.

8.2  Aggregate Cap.  Except for the categories excluded in Section 8.1,
each party's aggregate liability arising under or related to this
Agreement shall not exceed the total fees paid or payable by the Company
to Vendor in the 12 months immediately preceding the event giving rise
to the claim. For Vendor's liability arising from Data Breaches and
IP infringement, the aggregate cap is the greater of: (a) 12-month
trailing fees, or (b) USD $5,000,000.

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
ARTICLE 9 – INSURANCE
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

9.1  Required Coverages.  During the term of this Agreement and for
two (2) years thereafter (to the extent commercially available on a
claims-made basis), Vendor shall maintain the following insurance:
  (a)  Commercial General Liability: USD $2,000,000 per occurrence /
       USD $4,000,000 aggregate. Company is named as Additional Insured.
  (b)  Cyber Liability and Technology E&O: USD $5,000,000 per occurrence /
       USD $5,000,000 aggregate. Coverage includes first-party breach
       response, third-party liability, and regulatory defense.
       NOTE: See Exhibit C Insurance Requirements — the Company's
       Procurement Playbook (Section 7.1.2) requires USD $10,000,000
       aggregate for Tier 1 vendors. NovaTech is classified as Tier 2
       for insurance purposes effective this Agreement; Cyber coverage
       is set accordingly at USD $5,000,000 aggregate. If NovaTech
       is reclassified to Tier 1, coverage limits must be increased
       within 60 days of reclassification notice.
  (c)  Workers' Compensation: Statutory limits.
  (d)  Professional Liability (E&O): USD $2,000,000 per claim /
       USD $2,000,000 aggregate.

9.2  Evidence of Insurance.  Vendor shall furnish ACORD 25 certificates
of insurance to the Company before execution of this Agreement and
within 5 business days of each annual policy renewal. Certificates
shall evidence Additional Insured endorsements as required.

ARTICLE 10 – GENERAL PROVISIONS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

10.1  Governing Law.  This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of
the State of Delaware without regard to its conflict of law principles.

10.2  Dispute Resolution.  Disputes below USD $250,000 shall be
submitted to AAA Commercial Arbitration (Delaware rules). Disputes at or
above USD $250,000 may be litigated in federal or state courts of Delaware.

10.3  Entire Agreement.  This Agreement, together with all executed SOWs,
the DPA, and the Company's Procurement Policy Playbook (as incorporated
herein by reference), constitutes the entire agreement of the parties and
supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, and understandings.

10.4  Amendments.  Amendments require written agreement signed by
authorized representatives of both parties.

10.5  Severability.  If any provision is held unenforceable, the
remaining provisions continue in full force and effect.

10.6  Waiver.  Failure by either party to enforce any provision of this
Agreement shall not constitute a waiver of future enforcement rights.

10.7  Notices.  All formal notices must be sent by certified mail or
overnight courier to the addresses in the preamble, or via email to
legal@greybeard.com (Company) and legal@novatechsolutions.com (Vendor).

10.8  Force Majeure.  Neither party shall be liable for delays or
failures caused by events beyond its reasonable control (natural
disaster, war, pandemic, government action), provided it gives prompt
notice and uses reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact.

10.9  Anti-Corruption.  Vendor represents and warrants compliance with
all applicable anti-bribery and anti-corruption laws, including the
US FCPA and the UK Bribery Act 2010.

10.10  Export Controls.  Vendor shall comply with all applicable US and
international export control laws and shall not export, re-export, or
transfer Company technology in violation of such laws.
