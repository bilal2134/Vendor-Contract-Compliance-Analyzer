SECTION 1 – PURPOSE AND SCOPE
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1.1  Purpose
This Procurement Policy Playbook ("Playbook") establishes the minimum
contractual, operational, and compliance requirements that every vendor
providing goods or services to Greybeard Corporate Solutions ("Company")
must satisfy before onboarding and on a continuous basis throughout the
engagement. The Playbook applies to all third-party relationships with
total contract value exceeding USD $50,000 or any engagement that
involves access to Company systems, data, or facilities regardless of
value.

1.2  Scope of Application
This Playbook applies to:
  (a)  Information Technology and Software-as-a-Service vendors
  (b)  Professional services firms (consulting, legal, accounting)
  (c)  Cloud infrastructure and hosting providers
  (d)  Data processing and analytics service providers
  (e)  Managed security service providers (MSSPs)
  (f)  Any subcontractor performing services that account for more than
       20% of a prime vendor's deliverables under a Company contract.

1.3  Hierarchy of Documents
Where a vendor engagement involves multiple governing documents, the
order of precedence shall be: (1) applicable law and regulation,
(2) this Playbook as incorporated by reference into the executed MSA,
(3) the signed MSA, (4) any applicable Statement of Work or Order Form,
(5) vendor standard terms and conditions. In the event of conflict,
higher-ranked documents prevail.

SECTION 2 – DEFINITIONS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

"Affiliate" means any entity that directly or indirectly controls, is
controlled by, or is under common control with a party.

"Company Data" means any data, records, or information owned by or
entrusted to the Company, including Personal Data, financial records,
intellectual property, trade secrets, and Confidential Information.

"Confidential Information" means non-public information disclosed by
one party to the other that is designated confidential or that would
reasonably be understood to be confidential given the nature of the
disclosure.

"Critical Vendor" means a vendor whose failure to deliver would cause
material operational disruption to Company business operations. The
Vendor Risk Committee designates Critical Vendor status annually.

"Data Breach" means any unauthorized access to, disclosure of, or
destruction of Personal Data or Confidential Information, whether
accidental or deliberate.

"Personal Data" has the meaning ascribed to it under applicable
privacy law, including but not limited to the California Consumer
Privacy Act (CCPA), the EU General Data Protection Regulation
(GDPR), and the UK Data Protection Act 2018.

"Sensitive Personal Data" means Personal Data revealing racial or
ethnic origin, political opinions, religious or philosophical beliefs,
trade union membership, genetic or biometric data, health data, data
concerning a natural person's sex life or sexual orientation, or
precise geolocation data.

"SLA" means Service Level Agreement, a schedule or exhibit setting
measurable performance commitments by the vendor.

"SOW" means Statement of Work, a discrete project or service scope
document executed under the MSA.

SECTION 3 – GOVERNANCE AND APPROVAL THRESHOLDS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

3.1  Vendor Classification Tiers

  Tier 1 – Strategic Partner  (TCV > USD $5M or Critical Vendor status)
    Approval: Chief Procurement Officer + General Counsel + CISO
    Review cycle: Annual comprehensive risk assessment

  Tier 2 – Preferred Vendor   (TCV USD $500K – $5M)
    Approval: VP Procurement + Legal Counsel
    Review cycle: Annual self-assessment questionnaire

  Tier 3 – Approved Vendor    (TCV USD $50K – $500K)
    Approval: Director of Procurement
    Review cycle: Biennial risk review

3.2  Exceptions and Escalations
Any exception to a Tier 1 requirement must be approved in writing by
the Chief Procurement Officer and the Chief Information Security
Officer. Exceptions shall be documented in the Vendor Risk Register
with a specific remediation timeline not to exceed 180 days.

3.3  Disqualification Criteria
Vendors shall be disqualified from consideration if they appear on:
  (a)  OFAC Specially Designated Nationals list
  (b)  EU Consolidated Sanctions list
  (c)  Any applicable export control debarment list
  (d)  GSA Excluded Parties List System (EPLS)

SECTION 4 – MASTER SERVICE AGREEMENT REQUIREMENTS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

4.1  Mandatory MSA Clauses
Every MSA with a vendor covered by this Playbook must contain the
following provisions as non-negotiable terms:

  4.1.1  Governing Law and Jurisdiction
         All MSAs shall be governed by the laws of the State of Delaware,
         United States. Disputes below USD $250,000 shall be resolved by
         AAA Commercial Arbitration. Disputes at or above USD $250,000 may
         be litigated in federal or state courts of Delaware.

  4.1.2  Entire Agreement and Integration Clause
         The MSA, together with its exhibits and incorporated Playbook
         requirements, constitutes the entire agreement and supersedes all
         prior negotiations and representations.

  4.1.3  Assignment
         Vendors may not assign, transfer, or delegate any rights or
         obligations under the MSA without prior written consent of the
         Company, which shall not be unreasonably withheld. Any purported
         assignment in violation of this clause is null and void.

  4.1.4  Change of Control
         Vendor must provide at least 90 days advance written notice of a
         planned change of control event (merger, acquisition, sale of
         substantially all assets). The Company shall have the right to
         terminate the MSA without penalty within 60 days of such notice
         if the acquirer is a direct competitor of the Company.

  4.1.5  Limitation of Liability
         For Tier 1 vendors: Company's aggregate liability is capped at
         total fees paid in the 12 months preceding the claim. Vendor's
         aggregate liability is capped at the greater of: (a) total fees
         paid in the 12 months preceding the claim, or (b) USD $5,000,000.
         Liability caps do not apply to: breaches of confidentiality,
         intellectual property infringement, data breaches, gross
         negligence, or intentional misconduct.

  4.1.6  Warranty
         Vendor warrants that: (a) services will be performed in a
         professional and workmanlike manner consistent with industry
         standards; (b) deliverables will conform to agreed specifications
         for 90 days after acceptance; (c) vendor has the right to grant
         all licenses required by the MSA; (d) services and deliverables
         will not contain malware, back doors, or undisclosed data
         collection mechanisms.

  4.1.7  Indemnification
         Vendor shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Company
         against third-party claims arising from: (a) vendor's gross
         negligence or willful misconduct; (b) infringement of intellectual
         property rights by vendor deliverables; (c) vendor's violation of
         applicable law; (d) any Data Breach caused by vendor's acts or
         omissions.

4.2  Commercial Terms

  4.2.1  Payment Terms
         Standard payment terms are Net-45 days from receipt of a
         compliant invoice. Invoices must reference the Company purchase
         order number and SOW number. Late payment interest is capped at
         the lesser of 1.5% per month or the maximum rate permitted by law.

  4.2.2  Price Stability
         Fixed-price engagements are not subject to unilateral price
         adjustments during the agreed term. Time-and-materials rates
         may be adjusted annually with 90 days written notice, capped at
         the prior year's change in the US Consumer Price Index (All Urban
         Consumers) plus 2 percentage points.

  4.2.3  Most Favored Customer
         For Tier 1 vendors, the Company shall be entitled to pricing no
         less favorable than the lowest pricing offered to any other
         customer with comparable volume and contractual commitments.

4.3  Intellectual Property

  4.3.1  Work Product Ownership
         All custom deliverables, inventions, works of authorship, and
         developments created specifically for the Company under a SOW
         shall be deemed works-made-for-hire and shall be owned by the
         Company. To the extent any such deliverable is not deemed a
         work-made-for-hire by operation of law, vendor hereby assigns
         all right, title, and interest to the Company.

  4.3.2  Background IP License
         Vendor grants the Company a non-exclusive, royalty-free,
         perpetual, irrevocable license to use vendor's pre-existing
         intellectual property embedded in deliverables to the extent
         necessary to use and exploit the deliverables.

  4.3.3  Open Source Disclosure
         Vendor must disclose all open source software components
         incorporated into deliverables and confirm that no open source
         license terms impose requirements inconsistent with the Company's
         commercial use of the deliverables.

SECTION 5 – DATA PROTECTION AND PRIVACY
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

5.1  Data Processing Agreement (DPA) Requirement
     POLICY REQUIREMENT §5.1: Every vendor that processes, stores,
     transmits, or may access Company Personal Data must execute a
     Data Processing Agreement before any Personal Data is shared.
     The DPA must satisfy the requirements of GDPR Article 28,
     CCPA Section 1798.100 et seq., and any other applicable privacy law.
     Execution of a DPA is a condition precedent to contract signature
     and vendor onboarding.

5.2  Data Processing Principles
     Vendors must contractually commit to processing Personal Data:
  (a)  Only on documented instructions from the Company
  (b)  For no purpose other than the contracted service
  (c)  With appropriate technical and organizational security measures
  (d)  Only by personnel subject to enforceable confidentiality obligations
  (e)  In compliance with applicable privacy regulations

5.3  Cross-Border Data Transfers
     Transfers of EU-origin Personal Data outside the European Economic
     Area require one of the following legal mechanisms:
  (a)  Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) as updated June 2021, or
  (b)  Adequacy decision by the European Commission, or
  (c)  Binding Corporate Rules approved by a supervisory authority.
     Vendors relying on SCCs must complete a Transfer Impact Assessment
     (TIA) and make it available to the Company on request.

5.4  Data Subject Rights Support
     POLICY REQUIREMENT §5.4: Vendors must support the Company's
     obligations to respond to data subject requests (access,
     rectification, erasure, portability, restriction) within 72 hours
     of Company notification. Vendors must maintain systems capable of
     identifying, exporting, and deleting individual data records on request.

5.5  Data Retention and Deletion

  5.5.1  POLICY REQUIREMENT §5.5.1: Vendors must adhere to the Company's
         Data Retention Schedule (DRS), a current copy of which will be
         provided at contract execution and updated when materially revised.
         Vendors may not retain Company Data beyond the retention periods
         specified in the DRS unless required by applicable law or
         regulatory mandate, in which case the vendor must promptly notify
         the Company.

  5.5.2  POLICY REQUIREMENT §5.5.2: Upon expiration or termination of the
         engagement, vendors must certifiably destroy or return all Company
         Data within 30 days of the effective termination date. Destruction
         must follow NIST SP 800-88 Rev. 1 standards for media sanitization
         (Clear, Purge, or Destroy as appropriate). A signed Certificate of
         Destruction must be provided to the Company within 5 business days
         of completion. Backup media must be overwritten on the next
         scheduled backup cycle, not to exceed 90 days.

5.6  Data Breach Notification

  5.6.1  POLICY REQUIREMENT §5.6.1: Vendors must notify the Company's
         Privacy Officer and Chief Information Security Officer within
         4 hours of discovering a Data Breach or suspected Data Breach
         involving Company Data. The initial notification may be informal
         (email or telephone) but must be followed by a written incident
         report within 24 hours.

  5.6.2  The incident report must include: (a) description of the nature
         of the breach; (b) categories and approximate number of
         individuals affected; (c) categories and approximate number of
         data records concerned; (d) name and contact details of the
         vendor's Data Protection Officer; (e) likely consequences of
         the breach; (f) measures taken or proposed to address the breach.

5.7  Privacy by Design
     Vendors must implement privacy-by-design principles in any new
     system or feature handling Personal Data, including data minimization,
     purpose limitation, storage limitation, and pseudonymization where
     technically feasible.

5.8  Data Protection Officer
     Vendors processing significant volumes of Personal Data or processing
     Sensitive Personal Data on behalf of the Company must designate a
     Data Protection Officer (DPO) and provide the DPO's contact
     information to the Company's Legal team prior to contract execution.

SECTION 6 – INFORMATION SECURITY REQUIREMENTS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

6.1  Minimum Security Certification

  6.1.1  POLICY REQUIREMENT §6.1.1: All Tier 1 and Tier 2 vendors
         processing or storing Company Data must attain and maintain a
         current, valid SOC 2 Type II report (AICPA Trust Services
         Criteria: Security, Availability, and Confidentiality). The
         report must cover a minimum 12-month audit period and must have
         been issued no more than 12 months prior to contract execution
         and on each anniversary thereafter.

  6.1.2  POLICY REQUIREMENT §6.1.2: In addition to SOC 2 Type II,
         vendors providing cloud infrastructure services, SaaS platforms,
         or managed security services must also hold at least one of:
         ISO 27001:2022 certification (current and unrevoked) or
         FedRAMP Moderate authorization (for US government-adjacent workloads).

  6.1.3  Cloud service vendors hosting financial data or health data must
         additionally hold PCI DSS Level 1 compliance (for financial data)
         or HITRUST CSF certification (for health data) as applicable.

  6.1.4  Vendors must provide copies of all certifications and audit reports
         to the Company within 5 business days of request. Reports
         containing qualified opinions or exceptions must be accompanied by
         a management remediation plan.

6.2  Vulnerability and Patch Management

  6.2.1  POLICY REQUIREMENT §6.2.1: Vendors must operate a formal
         vulnerability management program. Critical vulnerabilities (CVSS
         score ≥ 9.0) must be remediated within 14 days of discovery.
         High vulnerabilities (CVSS 7.0–8.9) must be remediated within
         30 days. Medium vulnerabilities (CVSS 4.0–6.9) within 90 days.

  6.2.2  Vendors must apply operating system and application security
         patches within the timeframes above. Emergency out-of-band
         patches released by a vendor (e.g., Microsoft, Linux distros) must
         be evaluated within 48 hours and applied within the relevant CVSS
         timeframe.

6.3  Penetration Testing

  6.3.1  POLICY REQUIREMENT §6.3.1: Vendors must conduct annual external
         penetration tests of all internet-facing systems and applications
         that process Company Data. Tests must be performed by an
         independent third party (not the vendor's internal team). Results
         and remediation plans must be shared with the Company within
         10 business days of report completion.

  6.3.2  For Critical Vendors: Penetration tests must be conducted
         semi-annually (every 6 months). The testing scope must include
         web application testing (OWASP Top 10), network/infrastructure
         testing, and — where applicable — API security testing.

6.4  Access Controls

  6.4.1  Vendors must implement least-privilege access for all personnel
         accessing Company systems or data. Privileged access must be
         granted only to named individuals with documented business need
         and must be reviewed quarterly.

  6.4.2  Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) is mandatory for all accounts
         with access to Company Data or Company systems. SMS-based OTP is
         not an acceptable second factor for Tier 1 or Tier 2 vendors;
         FIDO2/WebAuthn or TOTP authenticator apps are required.

  6.4.3  All vendor personnel accessing Company systems must pass background
         screening appropriate to their access level before onboarding.

6.5  Encryption Standards

  POLICY REQUIREMENT §6.5: All Company Data must be encrypted:
  (a)  In transit: TLS 1.2 or higher; TLS 1.0 and 1.1 are prohibited.
  (b)  At rest: AES-256 encryption using industry-standard key management.
  (c)  Key management: Encryption keys must be rotated at least annually
       and managed separately from encrypted data (split key custody for
       Tier 1 data classifications).

6.6  Security Incident Management

  6.6.1  Vendors must maintain a documented Incident Response Plan (IRP)
         reviewed and tested at least annually. A summary of the IRP and
         the most recent tabletop exercise results must be provided to the
         Company upon request.

  6.6.2  Security incidents affecting Company systems or data must be
         reported per Section 5.6 of this Playbook (Data Breach Notification).

6.7  Security Awareness and Training
     All vendor personnel with access to Company Data or systems must
     complete annual security awareness training covering: phishing
     recognition, social engineering, password hygiene, data handling
     procedures, and incident reporting. Training completion records must
     be available for Company audit on request.

SECTION 7 – INSURANCE AND FINANCIAL ASSURANCE
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

7.1  Required Insurance Coverage

  POLICY REQUIREMENT §7.1: All vendors under this Playbook must maintain
  the following minimum insurance coverages and must name Greybeard
  Corporate Solutions as an Additional Insured on all policies except
  Workers' Compensation:

  7.1.1  Commercial General Liability (CGL)
         Minimum: USD $2,000,000 per occurrence / USD $4,000,000 aggregate
         Must include: products-completed operations, personal and
         advertising injury, contractual liability.

  7.1.2  POLICY REQUIREMENT §7.1.2 – Cyber Liability and Technology
         Errors & Omissions (Combined or Separate)
         Cyber Liability: USD $10,000,000 per occurrence / $10,000,000
         aggregate (Tier 1 vendors). USD $5,000,000 per occurrence /
         $5,000,000 aggregate (Tier 2 and Tier 3 vendors).
         Coverage must include: first-party breach response costs,
         third-party liability, regulatory defense and fines, business
         interruption, ransomware/extortion, and network security
         liability. Coverage must extend to vendor's subcontractors
         accessing Company Data.
         Technology E&O: USD $5,000,000 per occurrence / $5,000,000
         aggregate (Tier 1). USD $2,000,000 per occurrence / $2,000,000
         aggregate (Tier 2 and Tier 3).

  7.1.3  Workers' Compensation and Employers' Liability
         Statutory limits for Workers' Compensation.
         Employers' Liability: USD $1,000,000 per occurrence.

  7.1.4  Professional Liability (Errors & Omissions)
         Minimum: USD $5,000,000 per claim / USD $5,000,000 aggregate
         (Tier 1). USD $2,000,000 per claim / $2,000,000 aggregate
         (Tier 2 and Tier 3).
         Required for: professional services, consulting, managed services.

  7.1.5  Commercial Automobile Liability
         Required only where vendor personnel operate vehicles in
         performance of services. Minimum: USD $1,000,000 combined
         single limit.

7.2  Insurance Certificate Requirements
     (a)  Insurance certificates (ACORD 25 or equivalent) must be
          provided to the Company before contract execution.
     (b)  Certificates must name: "Greybeard Corporate Solutions and
          its Affiliates" as Additional Insured.
     (c)  Vendors must provide 30 days advance written notice of policy
          cancellation or material modification, 10 days for non-payment.
     (d)  Annual certificate renewal must be provided within 5 business
          days of policy anniversary.

7.3  Financial Health Monitoring
     Tier 1 and Critical Vendors must provide audited annual financial
     statements upon request. The Company reserves the right to require
     a performance bond or letter of credit if a vendor's financial
     condition materially deteriorates during the contract term.

SECTION 8 – BUSINESS CONTINUITY AND DISASTER RECOVERY
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

8.1  Business Continuity Plan (BCP)

  POLICY REQUIREMENT §8.1: Tier 1 and Tier 2 vendors must maintain a
  documented Business Continuity Plan. The BCP must address scenarios
  including: natural disaster, cyberattack, loss of key personnel, and
  supply chain disruption. Summary BCP documentation must be provided
  to the Company on request and must be tested at least annually.

8.2  Disaster Recovery Plan (DRP)

  POLICY REQUIREMENT §8.2: Vendors processing or hosting Company Data
  must maintain a documented Disaster Recovery Plan. Minimum recovery
  objectives for services hosting Company Data are:
    Recovery Time Objective (RTO):  ≤ 4 hours for Tier 1 services;
                                    ≤ 24 hours for Tier 2 and Tier 3.
    Recovery Point Objective (RPO): ≤ 1 hour for Tier 1 services;
                                    ≤ 4 hours for Tier 2 and Tier 3.
  Vendors must demonstrate DRP capabilities through annual DR tests and
  provide test result summaries to the Company within 30 days of the test.

8.3  Geographic Redundancy
     Tier 1 vendors providing hosted or SaaS services to the Company must
     operate from geographically redundant data centers in at least two
     distinct fault zones. Data centers may not share power grid,
     physical access routes, or cooling infrastructure.

SECTION 9 – SUBCONTRACTING AND SUPPLY CHAIN RISK
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

9.1  Subcontracting Approval

  POLICY REQUIREMENT §9.1: Vendors may not subcontract any portion of
  Company-related work without prior written approval from the Company.
  Requests for subcontracting approval must include: (a) subcontractor
  name and company profile; (b) scope of subcontracted work; (c) evidence
  of subcontractor's data security and compliance posture; (d) flow-down
  provision confirming subcontractor is bound by equivalent obligations.

9.2  Flow-Down Requirements
     Approved subcontractors must be bound by contractual obligations
     at least as stringent as those imposed on the prime vendor under
     the Company's MSA with respect to: data protection, information
     security, insurance, and confidentiality.

9.3  Fourth-Party Risk
     For Tier 1 vendors: vendors must identify and disclose to the
     Company any fourth parties (subcontractors of subcontractors) who
     may access Company Data and must confirm those fourth parties are
     subject to equivalent data protection obligations.

SECTION 10 – EXPORT CONTROLS AND REGULATORY COMPLIANCE
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

10.1  Export Control Compliance
      Vendors must comply with all applicable export control laws,
      including the US Export Administration Regulations (EAR), the
      International Traffic in Arms Regulations (ITAR), and equivalent
      non-US regulations. Vendors must not export, re-export, or transfer
      Company technology or data in violation of applicable export controls.

10.2  Sanctions Compliance
      Vendors represent and warrant that they are not on any applicable
      sanctions list (OFAC, EU, UN, UK HMT) and that they do not conduct
      business with sanctioned countries, entities, or individuals.
      Vendors must notify the Company within 24 hours if they become
      aware of any sanctions designation affecting the vendor or its
      key subcontractors.

10.3  Anti-Money Laundering
      Vendors must maintain anti-money laundering (AML) compliance
      programs appropriate to their business activities and must
      cooperate with any Company AML due diligence requirements.

SECTION 11 – ANTI-CORRUPTION AND ETHICS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

11.1  Anti-Bribery Compliance
      Vendors must comply with the US Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA),
      the UK Bribery Act 2010, and all other applicable anti-bribery laws.
      Vendors must not offer, promise, give, or receive, directly or
      indirectly, any bribe, kickback, or improper payment in connection
      with any Company engagement.

11.2  Code of Conduct
      Vendors must maintain a written Code of Conduct that includes
      prohibitions against: fraud, bribery, conflicts of interest, and
      human rights violations. Vendors must make their Code of Conduct
      available to the Company on request.

11.3  Whistleblower Protections
      Vendors must maintain a mechanism (hotline, email, or equivalent)
      for employees and third parties to report ethical concerns without
      fear of retaliation.

SECTION 12 – SERVICE LEVELS AND PERFORMANCE STANDARDS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

12.1  Minimum Uptime Commitment (SaaS/Hosted Services)

  POLICY REQUIREMENT §12.1: SaaS and hosted service vendors providing
  production services to the Company must commit to a minimum system
  availability of 99.9% measured monthly (excluding scheduled maintenance
  windows). Tier 1 SaaS vendors must commit to 99.95% availability.
  Availability calculations must exclude outages of less than 5 minutes
  and scheduled maintenance windows communicated at least 72 hours in advance.

12.2  Service Credit Regime
      Vendors must offer service credits for SLA breaches at a minimum of:
        95.0%–99.8% availability: 10% credit on monthly fees
        90.0%–94.9% availability: 25% credit on monthly fees
        < 90.0% availability:    50% credit on monthly fees
      Service credits are not the exclusive remedy for SLA breaches.
      The Company retains the right to terminate for convenience if
      availability falls below 95% in any two consecutive months.

12.3  Reporting
      Vendors must provide monthly SLA performance reports, including
      uptime metrics, incident summaries, and open problem tickets,
      within 10 business days of month-end.

SECTION 13 – AUDIT RIGHTS AND MONITORING
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

13.1  Audit Rights

  POLICY REQUIREMENT §13.1: The Company, or its appointed third-party
  auditor, shall have the right to audit vendor's facilities, systems,
  controls, and records relevant to the Company engagement no more than
  once per calendar year upon 30 days written notice (for Tier 1: once
  per calendar year; for Critical Vendors: up to twice per year). In the
  event of a suspected Data Breach or material compliance violation,
  audits may be conducted on 5 business days notice.

13.2  Cooperation
      Vendors must provide reasonable access to personnel, documentation,
      systems, and facilities necessary to conduct any audit under §13.1.
      Vendors may require audit personnel to execute appropriate
      non-disclosure agreements before commencing on-site activities.

13.3  Questionnaire Response
      All vendors must complete the Company's annual Vendor Risk Assessment
      Questionnaire within 30 days of receipt. Failure to respond within
      the stipulated deadline constitutes a material breach.

SECTION 14 – TERMINATION AND EXIT MANAGEMENT
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

14.1  Termination Notice Periods

  POLICY REQUIREMENT §14.1: Standard termination notice periods are:
    (a)  Termination for convenience: 90 days written notice from either
         party. This 90-day period is the Company's standard and must be
         reflected consistently across the MSA, any SOW, and any related
         vendor-issued order form or commercial schedule. Inconsistent
         notice periods across documents will be treated as a Policy
         Deviation and flagged for Legal review.
    (b)  Termination for cause (material breach): 30 days written notice
         with right to cure within the 30-day period; if breach is not
         cured in 30 days, termination becomes effective immediately.
    (c)  Termination for insolvency: immediate upon written notice.

14.2  Exit Assistance

  POLICY REQUIREMENT §14.2: Upon receipt of a termination notice,
  regardless of cause, vendors must provide at least 90 days of
  transition assistance at no additional charge, including: data export
  in standard machine-readable formats, documentation transfers,
  knowledge transfer sessions, and cooperation with successor vendors.

14.3  Survival Clauses
      The following obligations survive termination or expiration:
      confidentiality (5 years post-termination), data destruction
      obligations (30 days post-termination), insurance (to the extent
      claims may relate to pre-termination activities), and any
      provisions necessary to enforce surviving rights.

SECTION 15 – DOCUMENT RETENTION AND RECORDS MANAGEMENT
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

15.1  Company Records Retention Schedule

  POLICY REQUIREMENT §15.1: Vendors must apply the following minimum
  retention periods to all Company Data categories:

  +────────────────────────────────┬──────────────────────────────+
  | Category                       | Minimum Retention Period     |
  +────────────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────+
  | Financial records              | 7 years                      |
  | HR/employment records          | 7 years post-employment      |
  | Contracts and amendments       | 10 years post-expiry         |
  | Audit logs and access records  | 3 years                      |
  | Security incident records      | 5 years                      |
  | Customer/transaction data      | Per applicable law, min 5y   |
  | Email and correspondence       | 3 years for business records |
  | Backup media                   | 90 days rolling              |
  +────────────────────────────────┴──────────────────────────────+

15.2  Legal Hold
      When the Company issues a legal hold notice, vendors must
      immediately suspend destruction of any data covered by the hold,
      regardless of normal retention schedules, until the hold is
      formally lifted in writing by the Company's General Counsel.

15.3  Secure Disposal
      Disposal of physical media containing Company Data must comply
      with NIST SP 800-88 Rev. 1. A certificate of secure disposal must
      be provided to the Company within 10 business days.

SECTION 16 – ENFORCEMENT AND EXCEPTIONS PROCESS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

16.1  Non-Compliance Remedies
      A vendor's failure to meet any Policy Requirement identified in
      this Playbook may, at the Company's discretion, result in:
        (a)  Issuance of a formal Cure Notice requiring remediation
             within 30 days;
        (b)  Withholding of payment pending remediation;
        (c)  Suspension of work under affected SOWs;
        (d)  Termination for cause under Section 14.1(b);
        (e)  Recovery of damages attributable to non-compliance.

16.2  Exception Process
      A vendor seeking an exception to any requirement in this Playbook
      must: (a) submit a written exception request to Procurement with
      a detailed business justification; (b) propose specific compensating
      controls; (c) obtain sign-off from the CPO, CISO, and General Counsel.
      Approved exceptions are valid for one year maximum and must be
      re-evaluated at each contract renewal.

16.3  Playbook Amendments
      The Company reserves the right to update this Playbook at any time.
      Vendors will be notified of material changes with 60 days advance
      notice. For Tier 1 vendors, material changes that impose material
      new costs require mutual written agreement to amend the MSA
      accordingly.
