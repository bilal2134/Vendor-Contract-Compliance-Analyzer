SECTION C – ACCESS CONTROLS AND AUTHENTICATION
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

C.1  How does your organization control access to production systems
     hosting client data?
     ANSWER:
     - Role-Based Access Control (RBAC): All access is assigned based on
       documented job function. Least privilege is enforced; no default
       administrative access.
     - Privileged Access Management (PAM): Privileged accounts are managed
       through CyberArk PAM. All privileged sessions are recorded and
       subject to dual-approval for production changes.
     - Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): FIDO2/WebAuthn hardware security
       keys (YubiKey 5 series) required for all production access and
       administrative accounts. TOTP authenticator apps used for selected
       internal tools. SMS OTP is prohibited for any privileged access.
     - Quarterly Access Reviews: All production access is reviewed quarterly
       by system owners; stale accounts are disabled within 48 hours.
     - Just-in-Time (JIT) Access: Temporary elevated access is granted
       via JIT workflows with automatic expiry (maximum 8 hours).

C.2  Background screening for personnel:
     ANSWER: All employees and contractors with access to client data
     undergo pre-employment background screening, including: criminal
     history (7-year lookback), identity verification, education and
     employment history verification, and where applicable, credit
     history review. Screening is conducted by Sterling Talent Solutions.

C.3  Security training completion rate (last 12 months):
     ANSWER: 100% of personnel with access to client systems have
     completed annual information security awareness training, including
     modules on: phishing recognition, social engineering, data handling,
     password hygiene, incident reporting, and GDPR/CCPA awareness.
     Training platform: KnowBe4. Training completion records are
     maintained for 3 years and available for Company audit on request.

SECTION D – ENCRYPTION AND DATA PROTECTION
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

D.1  Describe your encryption standards for data at rest and in transit.
     ANSWER:

     In Transit:
     - TLS 1.3 is the default protocol for all external communications.
       TLS 1.2 is permitted for legacy integrations with documented risk
       acceptance. TLS 1.0 and TLS 1.1 are disabled at the network
       perimeter. Perfect Forward Secrecy (PFS) is required on all
       TLS configurations.
     - Internal service-to-service communication uses mTLS (mutual TLS)
       with certificate rotation managed by HashiCorp Vault.

     At Rest:
     - All production databases: AES-256-GCM encryption managed by AWS KMS
       with customer-managed keys (CMK). Keys are stored separately from
       encrypted data.
     - Object storage (AWS S3): Server-side encryption with AES-256-GCM;
       client-side encryption applied to sensitive datasets before upload.
     - Backup data: AES-256-GCM encrypted; tapes (offline cold storage)
       encrypted prior to physical transport.
     - Encryption Key Management: FIPS 140-2 Level 3 HSM (AWS CloudHSM).
       Keys rotated annually; immediate rotation upon suspected compromise.
     - Key custodians: 2-person rule enforced for master key access.

D.2  What encryption is applied to mobile devices and endpoint systems?
     ANSWER: All employee laptops use BitLocker (Windows) or FileVault
     (macOS) full-disk encryption tied to TPM 2.0 chips. Mobile Device
     Management (MDM) through Jamf enforces encryption policies on all
     company-managed devices.

SECTION E – INCIDENT RESPONSE AND BREACH NOTIFICATION
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

E.1  Do you have a documented Incident Response Plan?
     ANSWER: Yes. NovaTech's Incident Response Plan (IRP) v4.2 covers:
     detection and analysis, containment, eradication, recovery, and
     post-incident review. The IRP is reviewed semi-annually and tested
     through annual tabletop exercises.

E.2  What is your breach notification timeline?
     ANSWER: NovaTech is contractually committed to notifying the Company
     within 4 hours of discovering a Data Breach or suspected Data Breach
     involving Company data. This timeline is consistent with the DPA
     (Article 8) and the Company's Procurement Policy Playbook (§5.6.1).
     Internal escalation to the CISO occurs within 30 minutes. The
     dedicated incident response hotline (available 24/7) is:
     +1 (408) 555-0911.

E.3  Describe notable security incidents in the last 24 months.
     ANSWER: NovaTech experienced two security incidents in the last 24
     months involving customer-facing systems:
     (a) August 2024: Phishing attempt targeting 3 employees. One
         employee clicked a malicious link; MFA prevented account
         compromise. Investigation completed within 48 hours. No client
         data exposed. RCA completed; enhanced phishing simulation
         training rolled out.
     (b) February 2025: Vulnerability in a third-party library (CVE-2025-
         1843, CVSS 7.5) discovered. Patch applied within 19 days.
         No exploitation detected. Greybeard was notified as a precaution
         in accordance with contractual obligations.
     Neither incident involved unauthorized exposure of client Personal Data.

E.4  Tabletop exercise:
     Most recent tabletop exercise: September 12, 2025 (ransomware
     scenario). Executive summary available upon request. Action items
     from the exercise: (a) improve offline backup verification cadence
     (completed November 2025); (b) add automatic DNS sinkholing for
     known C2 domains (completed October 2025).

F.2  Disaster Recovery:
     ANSWER:
     NovaTech's SaaS platform is hosted on AWS in a multi-region
     active-active configuration (Primary: us-east-1; Secondary: us-west-2;
     EU data: eu-west-1).

     Committed Recovery Objectives (per contractual SLAs):
       Recovery Time Objective (RTO):  ≤ 2 hours (exceeds Tier 1 ≤4h standard)
       Recovery Point Objective (RPO): ≤ 15 minutes (exceeds Tier 1 ≤1h standard)

     DR Test: Most recent DR failover test completed November 20, 2025.
     Results: RTO achieved = 1 hour 42 minutes. RPO achieved = 8 minutes.
     Test report available upon request.

F.3  Geographic Redundancy:
     Production workloads run across two AWS Availability Zones per region.
     Primary and secondary regions are geographically separated (US East
     and US West) with independent power, cooling, and network infrastructure.
     EU customer data is hosted in eu-west-1 (Ireland) with eu-central-1
     (Frankfurt) as warm standby.
